Introducing a new era of personal brewing. A DIY beer revolution has arrived, enabled by the Internet of Things.

New product development

  • Challenge

    Time to reinvent the home brewing market.

  • Solution

    Identify users, market research, viability, proof of concept and Technical validation.

  • Results

    A roadmap for building the world’s first fully automated brewing machine.

Business Challenge

The trend of home brewing and craft beer appreciation has been growing since the late 1970s.

As the world is constantly evolving, our client started brewing a bigger idea to revolutionise the market. They wanted to build a fully automated beer brewing machine.

What we did

Enabled started working on the project to take BrewArt’s original prototype further.

Consumer experience was identified as key here so we identified and profiled the ideal user:

“Bart is a home brewing enthusiast who likes to experiment and experience different beer flavours at home. He values quality, technology and lifestyle. Bart wishes there was a better way to brew his beer without having to wash and disinfect every single plastic bottle every time.”

  • Journey mapping
  • R & D
  • Concept creation
  • Technology audit
  • Prototyping
  • Business modeling

Bringing it together

To match what Bart wants, we delivered a proof of concept for a complete BrewArt experience. As the product’s name suggests, the brand needs to exude elegance, sophistication and inspiration.

After considering the human factors, Enabled then delved into the technical and business sides of things. This stage involved answering questions such as “What technology would make it possible?”, “Is it a viable option?”, “How do we scale growth in the future?”

Results

From a rough prototype of a “beer machine,” BrewArt partnered with us to make the world’s first fully automated brewing system truly outstanding. With an Internet of Things feature, the product provides a seamless experience, backed by Omni-channel Retail and engaging User Experience design.

Internet of things

  • Challenge

    Minimise user effort for a stress-free experience.

  • Solution

    Control from mobile app.
    Sophisticated integration.

  • Results

    Seamless, frictionless monitoring of brewing process anywhere.

Business Challenge

How could users be confident that they will get their beer after more than 2 weeks of brewing? There are many factors that could go wrong, e.g. timing, temperature. We want to minimise human error arising from manual control.

The idea is to let homebrewers monitor the brewing process without having to micro-manage it.

What we did

We made two pieces of hardware – BeerDroid and your mobile device - talk to each other. To enable the Internet of Things, we built a mobile app on Apple’s iOS platform to capture data from the BeerDroid.

Additionally, when BrewArtists buy a recipe - a BrewPrint - from the online store (See more here), it automatically programs the BeerDroid to brew beer accurate to that style.

Precision is key. The app needs to take care of the “science” so that home brewers can express themselves with the “art.” Enabled got to learn the brewing process and translate that knowledge into the multi-functional mobile app.

Steps taken

  • Process mapping
  • Solution design
  • UX & UI design
  • App development
  • Systems integration

Results

The added digital component to a physical product improves user experience by allowing a “set-and-forget” process. No more fuss over the wrong temperature. No more missed milestones: the BeerDroid will tell you via mobile phone and push notifications. BrewArtists enjoy a frictionless brewing process with remote control anywhere anytime.

Omni-channel retail

  • Challenge

    Consolidate variety of ingredients for different recipes.

  • Solution

    A unitised ingredient system, complemented by an online store that works across web and mobile.

  • Results

    Intuitive shopping experience and reduced wastage in supply chain.

Business Challenge

Currently on the market, DIY brewing recipes usually contain the exact weight of different ingredient types (e.g. malt, yeast, malt extract). This might create wastage if homebrewers don’t use up the amount of packaged ingredients in one recipe. Also, the fixed system doesn’t allow creative freedom for BrewArt’s target users.

What we did

With 35 ingredients, we developed a unitised system where each recipe contains building blocks that can be shared among recipes. Think of each recipe as a Lego model.

BrewArtists can play with endless possible combinations of ingredients, and recreate their favourite beer styles from around the world.

Steps taken

  • Process mapping
  • Solution design
  • UX & UI design
  • App development
  • Systems integration

Meanwhile, this will also help simplify our client’s inventory management, since they only have to make sure the 35 core ingredients are in stock.

To complement the new system, Enabled also developed an online store that works across web and mobile.

The store has an extensive product database from recipes (BrewPrints), ingredients, to accessories as well as hardware.

Results

The unitised system not only makes sense in terms of supply chain management, but also provides a more intuitive shopping experience. BrewArtists can shop anytime anywhere and track the order status on any supported device.
