In The Field - Case Study

Royal Automobile Association (RAA)

RAA was founded in 1903 and more than half of South Australiaâ€™s drivers are Members. They offer many benefits to members including Road Service with Patrols responding to over 600,000 call-outs each year.

When the RAA needed help, they called us.

Enabled created DiscoVan & RSTI, two Apps that are used in every one of the Road Service Patrols.

RAA had recently found themselves in the awkward predicament of running short of a touch sensitive overlay technology that turned a small Windows CE devices into touchscreen. To the extent that if too many more failed they would not have an appropriate working solution for dispatching Road Service Patrols.

RAA selected Enabled to conduct a consulting piece to determine the requirements and feasibility of using an Android Tablet App to replace this old touchscreen in their Road Service Patrols.

After conducting this consulting Enabled was then engaged to build a replacement app on the Android platform.


DiscoVan

Codenamed DiscoVan, this Android Tablet App replaced the old Windows CE device used by the Patrol to both receive job dispatch information and feedback the status of their job to the Dispatch Processing Centre at RAA. To make this solution work also required Enabled to develop an IIS application to route their proprietary message format from PULSE (their dispatch and scheduling system) to the Android Tablets. This IIS application had the secondary function of maintaining constant connection of the tablets to the PULSE system.

Moving to a larger 10 inch screen also allowed for a better user experience by using the additional screen size to better present information. Enabled’s proven understanding of how people engage with touchscreens also provided numerous other improvements to the workflow allowing the reduction in time taken to conduct many activities.


At the time, the Panasonic Toughpad was identified as the most appropriate device as it could withstand significant heat and rough treatment expected in the environment of a Road Patrol vehicle. More recently RAA asked Enabled to develop a smaller mobile version of the App for their regional Patrols to use on BYO mobile phones. This also provides RAA's system additional redundancy as the metro Patrols can also use this version on mobile phones.


RSTI

(Road Side Tax Invoicing)

RSTI was a second Android Tablet App that Enabled were contracted to build. This replaced an aging Windows XP tablet containing a Sliverlight application at the rear of the Patrol. This App is used by the Patrol to bill customers when they purchased replacement items like car batteries. This application also interfaces with a portable combined receipt printer and credit card reader device via Bluetooth.

Significant reverse engineering of the undocumented source code was required to make this application communicate to their server. The SOAP protocol used by the system did not play well with Android and this presented additional challenges that were overcome by Enabled’s development team.


The Key areas that Enabled brought our expertise to were Consultancy, UX, Design, Mobile, Systems Integration but these projects had many elements including:

  • Feasibility of interfacing Android Apps to two existing but separate legacy systems.
  • User Experience Design to enhance the workflow of the apps.
  • DiscoVan - Android tablet app for receiving and communicating instructions and status between central Dispatch Processing Centre and Patrols.
  • IIS application to route two way legacy messaging protocol between server and devices.
  • Keeping tablets connected at all times via a special Keep Alive function.
  • RSTI - A Point of Sales and stock control application for retailing items such as batteries.
  • Interfacing Android tablets to a portable Bluetooth printer / credit card reader.
