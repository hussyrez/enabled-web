Royal Automobile Association (RAA)

RAA was founded in 1903 and more than half of South Australia’s drivers are Members. They offer many benefits to members including Road Service with Patrols responding to over 600,000 call-outs each year.

Customer engagement and loyalty

Business Challege

In responding to changes within the automotive industry, RAA has been working to strengthen its brand positioning “There’s more to RAA than you might think.”

If our previous projects with RAA focused on internal end-users, this project was about increasing value and engagement for external end-users, i.e. RAA’s members. Moreover, this should also help the company’s branding objective: to be seen as innovative and progressive.


Exploration

We conducted a Jobs To Be Done workshop to crystallise our shared understanding of user needs. Members needed a more convenient way to access member offers on the go, while not having to carry a physical member card everywhere.


Codenamed DiscoVan, this Android Tablet App replaced the old Windows CE device used by the Patrol to both receive job dispatch information and feedback the status of their job to the Dispatch Processing Centre at RAA. To make this solution work also required Enabled to develop an IIS application to route their proprietary message format from PULSE (their dispatch and scheduling system) to the Android Tablets. This IIS application had the secondary function of maintaining constant connection of the tablets to the PULSE system.

Moving to a larger 10 inch screen also allowed for a better user experience by using the additional screen size to better present information. Enabled’s proven understanding of how people engage with touchscreens also provided numerous other improvements to the workflow allowing the reduction in time taken to conduct many activities.


Development

After coming up with a number of designs, Enabled worked with RAA to refine the mobile experience.

The Key areas that Enabled brought our expertise to were Consultancy, UX, Design, Mobile, Systems Integration but these projects had many elements including:

  • Feasibility of interfacing Android Apps to two existing but separate legacy systems.
  • User Experience Design to enhance the workflow of the apps.
  • DiscoVan - Android tablet app for receiving and communicating instructions and status between central Dispatch Processing Centre and Patrols.
  • IIS application to route two way legacy messaging protocol between server and devices.
  • Keeping tablets connected at all times via a special Keep Alive function.
  • RSTI - A Point of Sales and stock control application for retailing items such as batteries.
  • Interfacing Android tablets to a portable Bluetooth printer / credit card reader.
